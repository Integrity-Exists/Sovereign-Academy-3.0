Missed Court? Don’t Panic. Here's What to Do.

If you missed your court date — take a breath. You're not alone, and there are ways to fix it before it spirals. This page will help you act fast to minimize damage and avoid jail time.

🚨 Step-by-Step Action Plan

  1. Call the Clerk’s Office ASAP – Ask if a bench warrant has been issued. Write down who you spoke to and what they said.
  2. If there's a warrant, ask if you can turn yourself in and immediately request a new court date (called a "motion to quash warrant").
  3. File a Motion to Reschedule – Explain why you missed it (illness, transportation, no notice, etc.). Attach proof if possible.
  4. Show up immediately if the court is open — sometimes you can handle it same-day at the docket call.
  5. Contact your bondsman or pretrial officer if you have one — failure to appear can also trigger bond forfeiture.

💡 Tips

  • Act within 24–72 hours of the missed date if you can
  • Keep records of everything you file or submit
  • If arrested, politely tell the judge you intended to appear and are prepared now
