Courtroom Scripts for Warriors

Walking into court without a lawyer doesn’t mean walking in alone. These scripts give you the words — and the **confidence** — to stand tall, speak clearly, and make your record count. Use them as-is or ask Sage to tailor one to your case.

🎤 Opening Statement

“Your Honor, my name is [Your Name], and I am appearing today as a self-represented litigant. I’m here to ensure that the rights of myself and my child are protected, and to address the issues outlined in this case. I appreciate the opportunity to be heard.”

🚫 Objecting in Court

  • “Objection, Your Honor — relevance.”
  • “Objection — asked and answered.”
  • “Objection — hearsay.”
  • “Objection — assumes facts not in evidence.”

📣 Requesting Continuance

“Your Honor, I respectfully request a continuance due to [reason: medical, missing records, newly retained evidence, etc.]. I need additional time to properly prepare and ensure a fair hearing.”

📁 Filing a Motion

“Your Honor, I’d like to submit a written motion to [dismiss/remove/suppress/etc.], and I’m prepared to explain the basis for this request if the court is willing to hear it.”

🧠 Cross-Examining Witness

  • “Isn’t it true that…?”
  • “Were you present when…?”
  • “Can you provide any documentation to support that statement?”
  • “Have you ever made a report like this before?”

💬 Final Statement

“Your Honor, I’ve provided the court with facts, evidence, and lawful arguments. I ask only that this case be judged fairly, based on truth and justice — not bias or assumption.”
